Greek Character Options

Testing Greek letter substitutions in the "estiasi" wordmark. The word comes from Εστίαση (Estíasi). Some Greek letters have similar shapes but add subtle foreignness.

Original — No Greek
estiasi
Current wordmark. All Latin characters.
e-s-t-i-a-s-i
Option A — Greek ε (epsilon) for e
εstiasi
Only the opening letter swapped. Epsilon (ε) is visually distinct from Latin "e" — rounder, more open. Signals the Greek root immediately.
ε-s-t-i-a-s-i
Option B — Greek ι (iota) for both i's
estιasι
Both i's replaced with iota (ι) — no dot above. Cleaner, more minimal. The missing dots give it a subtle uniqueness without being obvious.
e-s-t-ι-a-s-ι
Option C — Greek ε + ι (epsilon + both iotas)
εstιasι
The opening epsilon plus dotless iotas. Three Greek characters woven in. Most distinctive — clearly signals Greek origin while remaining readable.
ε-s-t-ι-a-s-ι
Option D — Greek τ (tau) for t + ι for i's
esτιasι
Tau (τ) replaces t — very similar shape in this font but the crossbar may differ slightly. Combined with dotless iotas.
e-s-τ-ι-a-s-ι
Option E — Full Greek transliteration
εστιασι
All characters replaced with Greek equivalents. Fully Greek but may not render correctly in Boska if the font doesn't include Greek glyphs. Also might be unreadable to non-Greek speakers.
