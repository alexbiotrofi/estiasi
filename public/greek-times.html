Times New Roman + Greek Characters

Times New Roman has full Greek glyph support. These are the actual glyphs from the same font — no fallback mismatch.

Option A — Greek τ + ι (tau + iota)
esτιasι
Tau replaces t, iotas replace both i's. Tau (τ) has a distinctly different shape from Latin t — curved descender, no crossbar. The iotas lose their dots. Three characters changed.
e-s-τ-ι-a-s-ι
Option B — Greek ε + τ + ι
εsτιasι
Epsilon + tau + iotas. Four Greek characters. Maximum Greek presence while keeping s and a in Latin for readability.
ε-s-τ-ι-a-s-ι
Option C — Greek ε + ι only
εstιasι
Epsilon for the opening, iotas for both i's. Latin t stays. Three characters changed — the most balanced mix.
ε-s-t-ι-a-s-ι
Option D — Greek ι only (dotless i's)
estιasι
Just the two i's replaced with iota. Most subtle change — the missing dots are the only tell.
e-s-t-ι-a-s-ι
Option E — Greek τ + ι (just t and i's)
esτιasι
Same as Option A. The tau is the star here — its curved shape breaks the Latin rhythm. Combined with dotless iotas.
e-s-τ-ι-a-s-ι
Option F — Greek ε only
εstiasi
Just the opening letter. Epsilon signals Greek immediately. Everything else stays Latin. Most minimal change.
